The-Wealth-of-Classes----Introduction

huanmoon007
2024-05-20

Recently, I’m leaving my junior high school for some reasons, and I’m trying to recall my school life in junior high school, I found that there’s a big difference between my high school and primary schoo—-the trade. In my primary school, since the headteacher’s strict control, no one would even bring a packet of snacks, as well as money. Therefore, trade has no chance to occur in my primary school. However, when I entered the high school, the teachers here are not that strict, although some teachers don’t allow students to bring snacks and food, some naughty ones will still do that. This will bring trade, our topic today.

I started wondering, what are the differences between the class trade and the trade in the real society? In order to answer this question, I discussed this question with some of my friends, and I also thought it by myself for a while. It will be much more interesting if I am able to write a couple of articles to express my opinion about it. Fortunately, I actually do since I got a blog.

Thus, I’m going to write a book consisted of numerous articles to answer this question, with my personal observation and ideas about class trade. Absolutely, these articles will be about economics, and there’s a famous economics book called the Wealth of Nations, so I named the book The Wealth of Classes.

The book will be divided to three parts:

  • BOOK I: The essence of class trade

  • BOOK II: The adjustment of class trade

  • BOOK III: The trade between distinct classes

Each of them might be consisted of 3 to 5 chapters, and I will discuss the theory of class trade in them.

Overall, just go and read the first chapter to enter the world of class trade!
